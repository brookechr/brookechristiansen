Extreme Events Engineering VIP Project

I am going into my third and final semester of being involved in this Vertically Integrated Project (VIP) under Dr. Jorge Macedo.

During my first semester, I was tasked with updating/fixing a legacy Python codebase for performing seismic catalog declustering calculations on a dataset of earthquakes in South America using the Zaliapin and Ben-Zion method that had gone untouched for the past 5 years. I knew very little about earthquakes or the formulas associated with classifying them, but was working with someone who had geophysics experience (though not a software development background). With all that said, it was challenging and sometimes frustrating to work with. However, with a lot of patience and Googling, I was able to make it work and add some new visualization functionality.

During my second semester, I created a MATLAB implementation of the Zaliapin and Ben-Zion method with more complex visualizations and also started creating a MATLAB k-means declustering implementation.
