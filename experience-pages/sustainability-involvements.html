Sustainability Involvements

Veggie Jackets

I'm currently serving as president for the Georgia Tech Veggie Jackets (our plant-based diet/lifestyle club), which has around 600 members in our GroupMe chat. My responsibilities include managing general and executive meetings, volunteer days, and communication with dining services.

We hold 2+ events open to general members per week (1 general meeting, fundraiser, or activism event and 1 group lunch), are currently campaigning for a local business near campus to add vegan options to their menu, work with the Humane League on ‘fast actions’ like calling businesses to promote humane consumer options, and work to foster a community where anyone at Georgia Tech who is interested in reducing their animal product consumption feels supported.

My proudest individual achievement/project as president is probably the GA Veg Orgs Network, which has required reaching out to and organizing clubs with similar purposes to Veggie Jackets around Georgia. I have linked organizations from Georgia State University, Kennesaw State University, and Fort Valley State University, and am in the process of reaching out to other schools nearby. With this network, we plan to share our events and advice, hold joint events, and inspire students at our schools to shift toward a vegan lifestyle.

I was formerly the outreach chair of Veggie Jackets, for which my responsibilities included planning meetings and volunteering events, advertising the club on social media, designing advertising materials, creating and maintaining consistent branding for the club, and reporting student concerns shared on social media to the school's dining providers.

The Humane League

I'm a trained volunteer for the Humane League, which is an organization that advocates for animal cruelty prevention, primarily for chickens, and works with Veggie Jackets to provide us with organizing resources and other guidance. My main form of participation is currently frequently doing actions on their Fast Action Network, such as emailing, calling, and filling out contact forms for companies to encourage them to adopt more humane policies.

Work on Climate

Work On Climate is an organization that helps connect people interested in contribution to climate change solutions. During late 2020 and early 2021, I did some pro-bono software development for Work on Climate on their Braintrust Slack bot. I'm currently just a general member, regularly participating in things like climate justice workshops, food waste reduction challenges, networking conversations, etc.

In December 2020, I contributed a blog post called Why Adopt a Plant-Based Diet? to the Pangolin website, an opportunity which I found since the creator of the website is a Work On Climate member.

I also was a software developer and graphic designer for the podcast Conversations with a Wannabe Scientist from Jan. 2021 to June 2021, which I also found since the podcast host is a Work On Climate member.
